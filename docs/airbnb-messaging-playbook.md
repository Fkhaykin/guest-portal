## Message lifecycle (all Poconos homes)

1. **Booking confirmation** (T#4 family, ~400 sends): thanks + "Can you please confirm the number of adults, children, and pets in your group?" Newer generation adds the comms timeline (registration 10 days prior → house manual 24-48h → check-in info morning-of) and guest portal link (guest.summitlakeside.com).
2. **~10 days out**: registration request — now always the guest portal link (guest.summitlakeside.com; the old per-home summitlakeside.com rental-agreement URLs are deprecated), due ≥5 days before check-in. Full names mandatory (gate rejects partials); vehicles can be "TBD" and added at the gate. Newer variants embed the $25/hr early/late checkout offer.
3. **T-3 days**: "Your stay is in just 3 days, how exciting!" — registration chase, escalating daily if unsubmitted, framed as protecting the guest ("I would hate for you to get stuck at the entrance gate").
4. **Morning of check-in**: the house-manual mega-message — gate address, door code, wifi, parking, hot tub/sauna rules, HVAC quirks, trash, quiet hours. Address and code are deliberately withheld until this point: "We don't release the address prior to day of check-in for the safety of current guests."
5. **Mid-stay**: reactive only (deliveries, maintenance, gate adds) plus proactive notices for burn bans, snow plowing, pool closures, water advisories.
6. **Night before checkout** (T#2/T#3, ~450 sends): 11am checkout; remove belongings (left items shipped with prepaid label + $50 processing fee); remove trash; report damages/citations (unreported citation +$25); leave linens/towels on beds or floor; late-checkout upsell $25/hr; leaving late without notice = $50 fee.
7. **Post-stay** (T#1, 466 sends): "If everything was to your liking, please consider leaving a 5 star review. Airbnb penalizes hosts for anything under 5 stars… If anything was not as described or unsatisfactory, please let us know so we can make it right." Plus the direct-rebooking hook: "feel free to email or text directly if you'd like to come back and we will extend a returning guest discount :)"

## Inquiry-type playbook

### Early check-in (the single most common ask)
- Standard 4pm (newer listings 5pm). **Current policy (2026): booked and paid in the guest portal under Add-Ons — $25/hr, max 2 hours (3pm or 2pm), $50/hr on holiday stays**, availability auto-gated on high-turnover days. Historical: $50 flat "2pm Early Check In" add-on, $75 noon, tiers up to $100.
- 3pm is usually granted free; anything earlier is always conditional on cleaners: "We're usually able to accommodate 3pm, but I'll need to confirm around 11:30am that morning once the cleaners have arrived for turnover."
- No prior-night guest → "the home is all ready so you are welcome to check in as early as you arrive :)" at no charge. We only charge when guaranteeing ahead of time.
- Won't stack a free early check-in AND free late checkout on the same stay.

### Late checkout
- Standard 11am. **Current policy (2026): booked and paid in the guest portal under Add-Ons — $25/hr, max 2 hours (12pm or 1pm), $50/hr on holiday stays**, availability auto-gated on high-turnover days. Historically 12pm was routinely comped on request; $50–150 flat tiers beyond that.
- Same-day turnover = hard no: "unfortunately we have another group checking in today so we would have to stick to the 11am standard check out."
- Evening checkout = book the extra night ("a 4pm checkout means we can not rent the home out for an additional day").
- Complimentary late checkout is the **default goodwill currency** — gifted for any service hiccup (gate trouble, snow, noise, cleaning issues) before cash is ever discussed.
- Overstays chased politely: "it's 20 past check out and our cleaners are waiting to get in… Could you please update me asap?"

### Discounts / price negotiation
- Peak/holiday weekends: polite firm no — "Unfortunately it is still a high demand holiday weekend for us so I wouldn't be able to provide a discount at this time."
- Otherwise: never argue numbers in chat; send an Airbnb **"special offer" at "our lowest possible rate"**, sometimes after "speaking to the owner" (the owner is the negotiating shield). Same-day-booking incentives ("if you are able to book today, I could extend $100 discount").
- Lowballs: decline kindly with the fee breakdown (Airbnb fees + cleaner + community rental fee = "we would lose money at $500 total") and redirect to a cheaper sister property.
- Standing discounts: **10% military/veteran with ID**; **~10% returning-guest discount** for direct rebooking (text/email, contact@summitlakeside.com); occasional birthday/loyalty gestures for well-reviewed guests.
- "Already discounted" deflection: "our pricing is automated based on timing and demand" / "there is already a last minute discount applied."
- Never hold dates: special offer expires in 24h, "the days will be available to others until a booking is confirmed."
- Cleaning fee never negotiable ("set by our cleaner").

### Pets
- Dog-friendly everywhere. **$100 flat fee per stay, covers up to 3 dogs** (not per pet); added via Airbnb change request if missed at booking; collected via Venmo/CashApp/Zelle when Airbnb tooling fails. Refunded if the pet doesn't come. Listed limit nominally 2 dogs <40lbs but flexed for well-reviewed guests.
- Service animals: no fee, but we ask "which disability service is the dog trained to provide" and that the dog isn't left alone. ESAs are treated as pets (fee applies) at most homes — practice varied; Mansion answers conflict on cats (charged $100 vs. excluded) — confirm before quoting.
- Vax docs are framed as no-stress: "they're really not strict — a vet receipt or even a photo of a dog tag will do," or leave the pet off the registration entirely.
- Allergy concerns: "we perform a deep cleaning with hypoallergenic materials after stays with pets."
- Pet fee never waived: "this is the fee we are charged by our cleaners, and disclosed on our listing… I am not authorized to waive or alter this fee."

### Gate / registration / occupancy (highest-volume operational topic)
- All Poconos homes are in Penn Estates (Mansion: Blue Mountain Lake). All guests check in at the **main gate, 525 Penn Estates Dr** — driver's license, printed gate pass. Cameras catch sneaks → "unreasonably high citation."
- Occupancy caps are framed as the permit, never preference: "We can not register more than 12 names… that is the occupancy limit for our permit to prevent parties and large gatherings." Cottage: 8.
- Flex levers, in order: infants/young kids left off the form; name swaps "on the back end" (just message the names); extra guests **$50/person** (max ~2 over, never at Lakehouse-style hard caps when gate-enforced).
- Vehicles never block anything: "you can add or change cars upon arrival. It's really only the guest names they require ahead of time."
- Guest stuck at the gate = drop everything: verify, call the gate, confirm back within minutes, apologize for the gate staff.
- Deliveries (DoorDash/Uber Eats/Instacart/flowers): guest messages the service name, we call in a pass — "Got it, I'll call them a pass now :)" Newer flow: register deliveries via the guest portal.

### Party screening / age policy
- Booker must be **21+**; if under 25, ask ages of all guests + explicit confirmation "this would not be a party and all house rules will be respected."
- All-under-21 or young birthday groups: flat decline — "I apologize but we can not accommodate this group."
- BBQs/small gatherings OK within the cap: "A BBQ is fine but we do not permit parties with loud music or excessive drinking."
- Airbnb's party-algorithm false flags: sympathize, blame the algorithm, redirect to direct booking ("search my user name and Poconos — it'll also be cheaper").

### Lake / boats / fishing / pools
- Every home includes free non-motorized boats (canoe + kayaks, pedal boat at Lakehouse; labeled racks, e.g. Manor's green canoe 288C on rack TL1, Chalet's red 81C on TL4). Life jackets in a closet, kid sizes included.
- The near lake is **boating + catch-and-release fishing only, no swimming** ("the lake is tested and treated for fishing, not swimming"). No license needed — private lake, "stocked with bass, pike, sunnies and catfish."
- Swimmable beach lake ~12-min walk; 2 community pools Memorial Day–Labor Day, 11am–7pm, free with amenity passes (12 on the oars by the entrance; replacement $75, raised to **$175** mid-2025).
- No gym/fitness room access for guests.

### Firewood / grill
- Firewood is never supplied: "There's often some left over from previous guests… Archie's Corner [community store] sells it, also Weis or gas stations." Recommend starter logs. 2026: firewood delivery is a paid guest-portal add-on (~$100).
- Propane for the grill IS always supplied, with a spare tank swapped on request. Indoor fireplaces are gas — keep the pilot lit.
- Burn bans announced proactively; fire pit suspended but grilling still OK.

### Hot tub / sauna
- Hot tub always on, year-round, ~101°F. Rules recited constantly: no food/drinks, rinse off lotions, lid closed, drop one chlorine cup (cabinet above the microwave) after each use, wait 1–2 hrs.
- First-line fixes by home: Mansion — "reset the breaker to the left of the tub"; low water — top up with hose/hot water; cloudy/foam — chlorine cup + 20-30 min.
- Saunas: Chalet infrared (90-min timer, 30–45 min warm-up, 120–140°F); Lakehouse 9kW (15-min max, clothes, no shoes); Mansion (15-min max, BT-AUDIO bluetooth). New installs are framed as unadvertised bonuses while flaky: "The sauna is not ready yet unfortunately, it is why we do not advertise it."
- Outages = proactive disclosure + binary choice: credit ($50–150) to keep the stay, or full refund to cancel — offered before the guest asks.

### Maintenance / in-stay issues
The pattern, every time: **apologize → thank them for reporting → try a remote/self fix → offer maintenance at the guest's convenience → follow up.**
- "Apologies for that! … Would you like them to stop by tonight or tomorrow morning?" Maintenance lives in-community: "Will be there within 5-10min!"
- Known self-fixes get walked through first (breaker box location, mini-splits must all match heat/cool mode or they error (L3/E7), game-room heater timer switch, router under the deer picture, fireplace YouTube short).
- Spares are kept and deployed fast (microwave, mini-fridge, JBL speaker, fans bought overnight on Amazon).
- 50-gal water heater explanation for hot-water complaints: "good for about 30 mins of showers, then needs about 30 mins to recover — space out showers."

### Cancellations / refunds
- Default: quote the policy, stay kind, redirect the mechanism to Airbnb — "you would need to contact Airbnb as they handle all payments and refunds" / suggest travel insurance for sympathy cases ("While I can not alter our cancellation policy, I would recommend contacting trip insurance").
- Accidental bookings minutes old: "if you cancel within the next 30min I will approve a full refund" (sometimes minus a small platform-fee charge).
- Weather: no host-side refunds while the home is usable — "You are not being asked to travel in conditions you believe are unsafe" — but goodwill comps offered (free early check-in/late checkout).
- Direct/Lodgify bookings: prepayments non-refundable; $500 security pre-auth 1 day before arrival, voided ~4 days after; partial credit offered only "if this weekend is rebooked by another guest."
- Host-side errors (double-bookings, sync glitches): instant ownership — full refund, or free upgrade to a bigger sister home framed as a win, plus apology.

### Damage & rule enforcement
- Honest guest reports of breakage: never charged — "Definitely would not charge you for a lightbulb… we err on the side of any small damage as cost of doing business."
- Suspected damage: neutral evidence-first question ("was there an incident with the grill during your stay?"), then documented Airbnb Resolution Center request with photos/invoices/citations. If the guest disputes with merit, back down gracefully ("I did confirm with maintenance there was a leak issue so I am going to cancel the charges, my apologies").
- When it turns hostile, state facts once and exit: "Everything was sent with documentation… I am not going to argue on here; Airbnb will look at the photos, invoices, and citations and handle as they see fit."
- Noise: "Please be sure to adhere to the posted quiet hours and keep noise down after 9pm. Please confirm receipt of this message." Quiet hours 11pm–7am everywhere.

### Complaints & review management (the compensation ladder)
1. Apologize specifically, investigate (cleaners/maintenance verify), fix same-day.
2. Comp time first (free late checkout), then targeted refunds (cleaning fee → $100–150 credit → up to ~50% of net for serious failures), then **20% future-stay discount**.
3. Proactive over-compensation for real failures: "We'd like to send you a $500 refund on your stay, as well as extend a 20% discount should you wish to book with us in the future."
4. Review recovery script: "To make it right, I'd like to refund your cleaning fee, and also offer a discount on a future stay so we can earn back those 5 stars. If you'd be open to holding off on a review until we get a chance to make this right, I'd really appreciate it." Posted bad reviews get a polite removal request citing "our small business."

### Cross-sell / direct-booking funnel
- Unavailable dates or wrong-fit groups always get redirected within the portfolio ("If you go to my profile…"), with honest amenity comparisons.
- Every happy checkout gets the direct-rebooking hook (returning-guest discount via text/email). Airbnb-blocked guests are steered to "search my username" / summitlakeside.com. On VRBO: "we aren't allowed to suggest going off platform on VRBO but are always happy to do so :)"
- Paid add-ons offered: early/late hours $25/hr, extension nights (often ~50% off), firewood delivery, picnic packages ($180/$250/$350, IG @deartable), extra guests $50/head.
